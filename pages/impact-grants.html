title: Engelhard Evidence of Impact Grants Criteria

We invite research project proposals from faculty and staff working within the Project to explore the impacts of their Engelhard course(s). Proposed projects can focus on student well-being, student learning, faculty experience, teaching and learning, campus community, or any Engelhard-related goal of your course. Preference will be given to proposals from interdisciplinary and/or cross-campus groups that could include faculty, campus resource fellows, and/or students. For the 2018-19 academic year we will award up to $10,000 total (with additional support from the Engelhard project team) between 3 to 6 projects.

Please submit your proposal by 5 pm on 9/5 to EngelhardProject@georgetown.edu. You will receive confirmation that we have received your proposal. If your proposal is accepted, an Engelhard team member will serve as a resource to each impact project team.

Eligibility

All faculty, all staff, all tracks, all ranks, part-time or full-time, involved in an Engelhard course in AY 2018-2019 are eligible to propose an inquiry project. We are especially interested in Engelhard Faculty Fellows and Engelhard Campus Resource Fellows who apply together.

Funding criteria

Proposals will be reviewed with the following funding criteria in mind:

  • Design a project that explores an impact of your Engelhard course. Impacts can be on student well-being, student learning, faculty, campus community, or any Engelhard-related goal of your course.
  • Proposals will be strengthened by inclusion of students on the research team.
  • Funds must be used for the approved activity.
  • Funds are for current use and must be used by June 15, 2019 or they will be forfeited.
  • This award has an expectation of manuscript development and submission by September 30, 2019.

Proposal Contents

Submit a Proposal that includes:

  • Proposed team details (names, titles, departments) and their roles on project
  • Project title, abstract, research question, hypothesis
  • Proposed design:
    • Course(s)
    • Method
    • Where will you find evidence of impacts?
    • Timeline
    • Itemized budget
  • Evaluation plan: Instruments to find evidence (consider using IRB project approved assessments)
  • Audience and dissemination plan


Proposals for AY 2018-19 are due on Wednesday, September 5 by 5 pm.

Please reach out with any questions to EngelhardProject@Georgetown.edu.
