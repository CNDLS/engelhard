title: Engelhard Conversations on Teaching

These conversations, which we fondly call Teaching to Mission, aim to connect the work we do as teachers and mentors to Georgetown’s mission and values. We began in Fall 2017 and have involved nearly 100 faculty at our Main, Med and Law campuses. This group reads The Jesuit Guide to (Almost) Everything by James Martin, SJ, and meets six times for discussion over lunch at the Jesuit Community. Co-facilitated with a Jesuit, this conversation group discusses Jesuit educational principles and facilitates reflection and sharing about the experience of teaching and working with students at a Jesuit institution.

Indicate your interest in participating by filling out our form. For other inquiries, please contact the Engelhard Project at engelhardproject@georgetown.edu.

What Participants Say

“I was very grateful to be welcomed and received into the home of the Jesuits and cared for with such attentive hospitality.”

“In a working environment in which many of us feel ‘one-offed’ or alone, this gathering created a real community in which gifts of consolation were shared over dinner and between meetings. I am not alone, and I am not alone in the experiences I have had. I feel socially supported and socially validated.”

“Creating a space where colleagues from across the university, in different roles, come together for an honest exchange focused on the spirituality of the university is very important. We instructors and staff often meet to discuss pedagogy (often just receive input, such as attending a workshop), or academic requirements of programs, or other institutional concerns, forgetting that there is a valuable spirituality present at Georgetown. Pausing to focus and learn about Ignatian spirituality, and reflect on what it means to be a Jesuit institution in action strengthens our ability to respond to students and each other.”
